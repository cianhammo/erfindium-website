The Problem

The biggest problem with invention disclosures isn't that they come in incomplete. It's that most of them never come in at all.

Engineers have ideas. But when disclosure means a long form, unclear questions, and the feeling that it will just generate follow-up work — they quietly drop it. Their invention stays in a notebook, or buried in an email chain, and the company loses valuable IP it never knew it had.

When disclosures do get submitted, they're often incomplete or inconsistent, which creates back-and-forth, slows down review, and adds overhead that compounds over time.

The Solution

Erfindium is a software product that makes invention disclosures painless for engineers to submit, and effortless for patent managers to review.

Engineers get a step-by-step guided submission. Reviewers get structured, complete disclosures — ready to evaluate on first read, with fewer follow-up questions.

The system supports the process around disclosure — clearer inputs, better organization, faster first-pass review — without replacing legal or IP judgement.

Lower friction Employees submit inventions without the usual process overhead.
Stronger inputs Improve the quality and structure of disclosures before internal review begins.
Less back-and-forth Reduce follow-up loops and make the first review step more efficient.
Better visibility A clear view of what's coming in and where attention is needed.

FAQ

Does this replace patent attorneys or legal review?

No. The system supports the disclosure workflow upstream of legal review, not in place of it. Attorneys and IP managers still make the calls.

Who handles the data? Is it kept private?

Disclosure data stays within the company's own deployment. The system is designed for a high-trust, legal-adjacent context where confidentiality is non-negotiable.

What does deployment look like?

The current model is a dedicated deployment per company, not a shared platform. Each client runs their own instance with their own credentials and data.

Is the product already live?

Yes, in a live pilot at a German engineering company, focused on validating workflow quality in a real industrial context.
